This Kaggle dataset contains information on restaurants available through DoorDash in Canada. It includes 3,920 observations (restaurants) and 10 variables that help identify and assess whether to order from each restaurant. The variables in the dataset are:

  • Restaurant name (categorical) The name of the restaurant.
  • DoorDash URL (categorical) A unique link to the restaurant’s DoorDash page.
  • Distance to order (numerical) Distance from specified point in the city to make an order (mile)
  • Star rating (ordinal) Customer star rating out of 5 stars.
  • Number of reviews (numerical) Number of reviews.
  • City (categorical) The city where an order placed.
  • Price range (categorical) Price range.
  • Cuisine/category 1 (categorical) Cuisine served at the restraunt.
  • Food specialization/category 2 (categorical) Food category/type served at the restraunt.

Question: What factors contribute to a higher overall rating for a restraunt on Door Dash?

This is an inference type question, with the main goal of identifying what aspects of a restraunt are associated with a higher Door Dash rating. In this context, a multiple linear regression could be the main model of interest for answering this quesiton.

1.¶

A multiple linear regression (MLR) model is an optimal method for analyzing the factors that contribute most to a higher overall star rating. Given that the target variable (star rating) is ordinal, ranging between 0.0 and 5.0, MLR is a suitable choice due to its ease of interpretation and ability to incorporate both numerical and categorical variables as predictors. Additionally, the model’s coefficients allow us to determine how each variable impacts a restaurant’s rating.

Assumptions of Multiple Linear Regression:

  • There should be an approximately linear relationship between the predictors and the star rating.
  • Observations should be independent of one another.
  • The variance of errors should remain constant across all levels of each predictor (homoscedasticity).
  • Predictors should not be highly correlated with one another (to avoid multicollinearity).
  • The residuals (errors) should be approximately normally distributed.

Possible limitations of MLR:

  • Star ratings can sometimes be interpreted as ordinal, which could violate linearity.
  • Omitted variables Bias due to the limited nature of the dataset.
  • Certain categorical features may be highly correlated, such as the price of a restraunt and the distance it is from the "city center".
